Hashimoto Disease: Natural Treatment for Better Daily Living

Hashimoto disease is a complex, multifactorial autoimmune condition affecting the thyroid. Discover how natural approaches can help restore gut health, reduce inflammation, and optimize thyroid hormone production for improved quality of life.

Hashimoto disease is a complex, multifactorial autoimmune pathology that affects the thyroid, an essential gland for our metabolism. It can cause fatigue, weight gain, mood disorders, and digestive discomfort. However, with appropriate management that restores the intestinal barrier, supports the immune system, regulates inflammation, provides proper nutrients, and addresses stress, it is possible to optimize thyroid hormone production and improve the comfort of life for those affected.

Step 1: Restore the Intestinal Barrier

A permeable intestinal barrier ("leaky gut") allows foreign molecules to pass into the bloodstream, triggering an excessive immune response. This is a key factor in autoimmune diseases like Hashimoto.

Gluten stimulates the secretion of zonulin, a protein that "opens" intestinal junctions and promotes inflammation.

Foods to Avoid:

  • Gluten-containing cereals: wheat, barley, rye, oats
  • Industrial products containing gluten (breads, sauces, flavored yogurts)
  • Dairy products (in case of lactose intolerance)

Step 3: Rebalance Nutritional Intake

To reduce general inflammation and support the thyroid:

Omega-3:

Consume small fatty fish (sardines, mackerel, anchovies) 1 to 3 times per week for their anti-inflammatory effects and their role on hormone receptors.

Attention to Goitrogenic Foods:

Certain raw foods consumed in excess can slow iodine absorption, essential for the thyroid:

  • Cabbage
  • Broccoli
  • Radishes
  • Cassava
  • Sweet potatoes

‚úÖ Tip: Consume them cooked and in moderate quantities, as part of a diverse and balanced diet.

Step 6: Manage Stress and Emotional Balance

Chronic stress is detrimental to intestinal health, the immune system, and hormonal function. It can become the breeding ground for an autoimmune disease like Hashimoto.

Recommended Activities:

  • Regular physical activity: Improves stress management and metabolism
  • Artistic or community activities: Promote expression and social connection
  • Brief therapies or EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing): Useful for people who have experienced deep stress
  • Mindfulness meditation and yoga: Strengthen resilience and calm the nervous system

There is no single answer, but a personalized approach according to each person's history, lifestyle, and psychology.
